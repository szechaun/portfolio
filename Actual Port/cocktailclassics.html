The Project:Cocktail.

Cocktail classics is an mobile app designed from scratch to teach new drinkers the necessary steps to create the famous classics.

This was created for a university group project in order to understand the principles of user interface design. The goal was to understand the necessary steps from start to finish defining goals, to doing market research, to creating iterative prototypes.

Tools

Adobe Illustrator, Sketch, ProtoPie

Skills

Graphic Design, Interface Design, Product User Experience, Prototyping

Video

The Process

Our project timeline was 3 weeks. My role for this project was mainly the developer interface designer. My partners Zax Chen and Liv Bennet were the main graphic and interface designers. As the developer, my responsibility for my team was to let my team know which interface was feasible and what’s not since I had little experience in mobile development.

The Problem

The course doesn’t teach how to make mobile app only how to design it and I was the only mobile developer and I wasn’t very experienced at it. With the scarce timeline given I had to figure out everything from the layout to implementing a sensor to the app as a mandatory requirement for the project. I could not use any mobile prototyping tool since a sensor was involved. However, In my attempt of desperately searching I had come across a tool called ProtoPie that allowed mobile sensor implementation in the prototype. This discovery freed up a lot of time resource that allowed me focus more on the interface design and time to conduct a heuristic evaluation to gain feedback on our product.

Iterations

For the first prototype, we were not required to have any of the visualization components yet and so I wanted to make sure we were getting all the sensor readings correctly from Arduino. We had 3 sensors sound, pressure, and galvanic. I made sure values were varying depending on the input. After that, our team just fit all the sensors and circuit into a demo housing to showcase that our sensors were working. Because all the sensors were working fine the feedback we received was that our project needed to be fitted into something sturdy that could take some abuse. So for the final iteration we made sure to have our project weighted down. We also linked the sensor reading to a visualization so users could easily follow their progress.

Reflection

During the course of this project, I’ve applied and extended a lot of what I have learned in Interactive Arts and Technology. The design process of ideating to market research and user testing. The takeaway for me in this project is that I shouldn’t panic if I do not know how to do something. There are a lot of resources everywhere and I just need to know where to look for it.

At the end at the final showcase, many people enjoyed our app and liked the interface. We received nearly a perfect mark for this project.
